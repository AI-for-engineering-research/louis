What I've found so far on clwc

The core finding (a correctness gap, not a tuning knob):

CRTM's WATER_CLOUD optical-property LUT is indexed by effective radius r_e (the ratio of the 3rd to 2nd moment of the droplet size distribution). The current BOWER_LIQUID scheme in effr.py computes

r = (3·LWC / (4π·ρ_w·N))^(1/3)

which is the mean-volume radius r_v, not r_e. I verified this numerically — the implementation's output matches r_v to the digit. The two differ by the Martin et al. (1994) spectral-dispersion factor k:

r_e = r_v · k^(−1/3), k = (r_v/r_e)³ < 1

So the current scheme feeds CRTM droplets that are 8% (ocean) to 14% (land) too small vs. the effective radius CRTM expects. Modern IFS/ECRAD uses the complete form:

r_e = [ 3·E_d·(LWC + RWC) / (4π·ρ_w·k·N_d) ]^(1/3)

with k = 0.67 (continental/land), 0.80 (maritime/ocean) from Martin et al. 1994, N_d ≈ 50 cm⁻³ ocean / 300 cm⁻³ land, and E_d = Wood (2000) drizzle/dispersion enhancement (≳1, matters when drizzle LWC > 5–10% of cloud LWC). The current code has k=1, E_d=1, and excludes RWC — i.e. it's the IFS formula stripped of exactly the three pieces that distinguish r_e from r_v.
