Snow Reconcile dynamic NWP snow with fixed land covers ▶

Mixing a fixed land snapshot with the NWP's time-varying snow needs assumptions: water cover is left unchanged (no snow settles on water), ice cover is zeroed (NWP snow is assumed to cover the dataset's ice), and snow cover is the NWP snow capped at the cell's non-water fraction. Ice compute_ice_reff — ECMWF IFS ice scheme ::EffectiveRadius ▶

The ECMWF ice scheme (IFS CY41R2, p.100) derives a temperature-dependent particle number from the difference between liquid and ice saturation pressures, then — assuming identical spherical ice particles — backs out a radius from the mass of one particle. Rain / Snow compute_rain_reff & compute_snow_reff — IFS exponential PSD ::EffectiveRadius ▶

Rain and snow share the ECMWF IFS hydrometeor scheme (p.96): an exponential particle-size distribution N(D)=N₀ exp(−ΛD), where the slope Λ comes from the specie mass and air density via Table-7.1 coefficients (rain vs snow differ only in na, nb, a, b). The effective radius is the 3rd/2nd-moment ratio, which for this distribution is 3/(2Λ).

$$ \Lambda = \Big(\frac{n_{a}\,a\,\Gamma(b+1)}{q\,\rho}\Big)^{\!\frac{1}{\,b+1-n_{b}\,}},\qquad r_{\text{eff}} = \frac{3}{2\Lambda} $$

Consistency cleanup_cloudvars — zero cloud fields where the cell is cloud-free ▶

NWP output is sometimes inconsistent: a cell may have non-zero cloud fraction but zero condensate, or vice-versa — both meaning "no cloud". HRRR Where this story branches — the model-level path

The same make_crtm_input runs for HRRR data, but several source-specific quirks flip on (all localized to HRRRAtmData / HRRRSfcData and a handful of guards in the main file):

  • Model levels, not pressure levels. p_layers/p_levels are 2-D (profile, layer), so the *_grid broadcasts are no-ops and absorbers interpolate via the 2-D path. ERA5's 1-D pressure axis is the simpler case shown in the main flow.
  • No ozone. HRRRv4 doesn't simulate O₃, so it's appended to the well-mixed gas list and filled from the US-standard-atmosphere CSV; the atm loop then continues past O₃ to avoid overwriting it with −1.
  • No surface winds. HRRR surface data lacks 10 m winds, so they're taken from the lowest atmospheric model level (level 1):
L256        self.wind_data = ds_atm.ds.sel({"valueOfFirstFixedSurface": 1})[
L257            vr.WindSpeed.hrrr_varname
L258        ]
  • Thompson schemes unlock. Because HRRR carries particle number concentrations (NCCICE/NCONCD/SPNCR), the THOMPSON8/THOMPSON28 radius schemes pass validate_compatibility for HRRR but are rejected for ECMWF. Those schemes route through upp_effr.get_effective_radius instead of the ECMWF/Bower closed forms in the main flow.

If the HRRR branch is worth its own full walkthrough (the upp_effr Thompson microphysics is substantial), it deserves a sibling flowchart — this callout marks the seam.
